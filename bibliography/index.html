Bibliography

These sources were used for story background, historical context, public-domain tale versions, and general fairytale research. Image files and advertisement graphics are stored locally in the website's assets folder.

Information Sources

  1. SurLaLune Fairy Tales. History of Cinderella. Used for Cinderella origins, variations, and cultural background.
  2. Cox, Marian Roalfe. Cinderella: Three Hundred and Forty-five Variants. Used for the idea that many Cinderella-style stories exist worldwide.
  3. SurLaLune Fairy Tales. Annotated Tales H-R. Used for Little Red Riding Hood background and comparison research.
  4. EBSCO Research Starters. Little Red Riding Hood. Used for a summary of the story's literary significance.
  5. Wikisource. English Fairy Tales: The Story of the Three Little Pigs. Used for Joseph Jacobs' public-domain version of the story.
  6. Stories Preschool. The Three Little Pigs. Used for brief historical publication context.
