About us

Why Joshi Journals exists

Joshi Journals is a student-made fairytale website designed to make classic stories feel clear, useful, and enjoyable for modern readers. Instead of only retelling the tales, the website explains where they came from, what their symbols mean, and why people still discuss them today.

Our Purpose

The project focuses on three famous fairytales: Cinderella, Little Red Riding Hood, and The Three Little Pigs. Each content page looks at the story from more than one angle, including historical background, symbolism, moral lessons, and modern interpretations. This helps visitors see that fairytales are not just simple children's stories. They are part of culture, education, family storytelling, and the way societies pass warnings and values from one generation to the next.

The design uses a custom logo, story images, warm colours, interactive navigation, rolling advertisements, and video spaces to make the website feel like a complete online publication. The overall style is meant to be friendly and polished while still being easy to read for school audiences.

Website Planning

Audience

The main audience is students, teachers, and families who want quick but meaningful background information about classic fairytales.

Structure

The website starts with a landing page, moves to a home page, and then links to contact, bibliography, evaluation, and three information pages.

Navigation

Main pages are linked from the top menu and story cards. These links use new browser windows to match the task requirement.

Original Design

The website includes a custom brand identity, styled buttons, banners, page layouts, and a custom video player area for the edited story videos.
